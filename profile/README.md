# [why not] Integration

We are **why not Integration gGmbH** — a non-profit organization based in Hamburg. We work to strengthen social cohesion and support the integration of refugees, migrants, and people with a migration background. Integration happens where people meet. That belief is at the heart of everything we do.

## Who we are

We are an NGO organized as a gemeinnützige GmbH. We are independent — institutionally, politically, and religiously. Our team of staff, volunteers, sponsors, and leadership brings years of experience in integration, diversity, and community work.

We are funded mainly through donations and private and public grants. Together, we build spaces where different people can meet, learn from one another, and feel at home.

## What we do

We work in three ways:

**We create places to meet.**  
Through our community spaces — especially our café in Ottensen — we offer room for conversation, culture, and everyday connection. Concerts, cultural events, language practice, and neighborhood activities bring people and cultures closer together.

**We offer counseling and education.**  
We run seminars, workshops, and discussion formats on integration, interculturality, diversity, and political participation. We also provide advisory and support services for refugees — including mobile counseling and short-term care for vulnerable people who need special protection.

**We strengthen community.**  
We bring people together across cultures and generations — in Ottensen, across Hamburg, and online. Our community garden, neighborhood programs, and shared spaces are open to everyone.

## Our café in Ottensen

Our café is our heart. It is a Wohlfühl-Café — a place to feel welcome — where neighbors, newcomers, long-time Hamburg residents, and visitors can sit down together. We believe in low-barrier access: solidarity pricing makes sure everyone can join, even when a regular cappuccino is out of reach.

**Address:** Daimlerstraße 38, 22763 Hamburg (Ottensen / Altona)

## Our projects

Beyond daily café life, we run social projects that support people with flight or migration histories in everyday life and empower them in their own plans. This includes emergency accommodation and first care for refugees with disabilities, help with registration, and support finding suitable housing.
